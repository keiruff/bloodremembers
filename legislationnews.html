Recent Reparations Legislation

Recent legislative efforts regarding reparations in the US include both federal and state-level initiatives. Nationally, H.R. 40 and S. 40 have been reintroduced to establish commissions studying reparations. In Washington D.C., a new law allows the city to explore reparations for Black residents impacted by slavery and Jim Crow.

Federal Level

  • H.R. 40: Commission to Study and Develop Reparation Proposals for African Americans. View Bill
  • S. 40: Senate companion bill to H.R. 40. View Bill
  • Senator Booker's Press Release

State and Local Level

  • Washington D.C.: A new law allows the city to study slavery’s legacy and develop reparations. Learn more
  • California: In 2024, the California Legislative Black Caucus introduced 14 bills tied to the state’s 2023 reparations report. Full list of bills

Other Key Points

  • Diverse Proposals: Reparations may include cash payments, land, education, or national apology. NAACP on Reparations
  • Ongoing Debate: Strong support from some legislators, but ongoing opposition remains. NPR coverage
  • Historical Roots: Rooted in the centuries-long legacy of slavery and racial injustice. Human Rights Watch
