A Timeline of African Greatness

Africa's story is not one of darkness or deficit — it is one of empire, scholarship, architecture, trade, and resistance.

c. 2400 BCE – Kingdom of Kush (Nubia)

Located in what is now Sudan, Kush rivaled Ancient Egypt and ruled over it during the 25th Dynasty.

c. 700 CE – Ghana Empire

One of West Africa's earliest empires, known for gold and salt trade.

c. 1235–1600 CE – Mali Empire

Home to Mansa Musa, the wealthiest person in history. Center of Islamic learning in Timbuktu.

c. 1464–1591 CE – Songhai Empire

Known for universities and advanced administration. Major centers: Gao and Timbuktu.

c. 1180–1897 CE – Kingdom of Benin

Renowned for bronze artistry and complex urban planning. Destroyed during British colonization.

c. 1100–1500 CE – Great Zimbabwe

Stone-built capital of a prosperous kingdom in southern Africa. Important trade center.

1452–1494 CE – Papal Bulls & Portuguese Slave Trade Contracts

Pope Nicholas V issued bulls like *Dum Diversas* and *Romanus Pontifex* granting Portugal the right to enslave non-Christians. These decrees enabled the Atlantic slave trade.
