c. 2400 BCE – Kingdom of Kush (Nubia)

Located in modern-day Sudan, the Kingdom of Kush rivaled Ancient Egypt in power and culture. It was known for its pyramids and iron production.

c. 700 CE – Ghana Empire

The Ghana Empire was one of the earliest known West African empires, dominating trade in gold and salt.

c. 1235–1600 CE – Mali Empire

The Mali Empire flourished under rulers like Mansa Musa and became a hub for Islamic learning and wealth.

c. 1464–1591 CE – Songhai Empire

The Songhai Empire surpassed Mali in size and influence. It was a powerful Islamic state with a strong military and education system.

c. 1180–1897 CE – Kingdom of Benin

Renowned for its sophisticated art, especially bronze and ivory, Benin was looted and destroyed during British colonization.

c. 1100–1500 CE – Great Zimbabwe (Mutapa Empire)

Great Zimbabwe was a monumental city of stone structures. It thrived on trade and architecture before European colonization.

1452–1494 – Papal Bulls Authorize Enslavement

Pope Nicholas V issued decrees like Dum Diversas and Romanus Pontifex, granting Portugal the right to enslave Africans.

1619 – First Enslaved Africans in English Colonies

In 1619, the first documented group of Africans arrived in Virginia, marking the beginning of slavery in what became the U.S.

1880s–1914 – Scramble for Africa

European powers carved up Africa during the Berlin Conference, disregarding indigenous nations and histories.
