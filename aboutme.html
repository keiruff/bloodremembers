About Me

I am a proud descendant of survivors — of stolen ancestors whose names, nations, and stories were nearly erased by slavery and colonization. My DNA tells a powerful story of origin and resilience: I carry blood from Nigeria (including Yorubaland), Benin-Togo, Cameroon, Mali, Central West Africa, and the Southern Bantu

My journey has also revealed Portuguese and Brazilian ancestry connected to regions like Minas Gerais and São Paulo, with echoes of Sephardic Jewish migration through Iberian exile and the transatlantic world. I even carry traces of Northeast Asian ancestry.

Through DNA matching, I’ve found connections to individuals buried in Rupert’s Valley on the remote island of St. Helena — victims of the transatlantic slave trade who were captured and detained after British “abolition.” These matches, some over 30%, are proof that my ancestors were among those ripped from their homelands, shipped across oceans, and dumped into forced labor or confinement under colonial control.

I created this space to honor their memory, demand justice, and reclaim truth. My blood remembers — and so does this work.
